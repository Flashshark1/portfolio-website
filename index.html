Week 4

During the discussion, we focused on integrating the farm monitoring system with the hydroponic setup. We sketched out the structure and explored various design ideas, each presenting unique advantages and challenges. Key considerations such as feasibility, scalability, and affordability were thoroughly analyzed. These discussions allowed us to evaluate the practicality of combining the two systems while ensuring the solution would be effective and cost-efficient.

Week 5

We discussed methodologies and preparations, focusing on equipment, techniques, and challenges. We planned to use IoT-enabled sensors for real-time monitoring of soil and hydroponic conditions, incorporating microcontrollers and communication modules. Preparations included prototyping, sensor calibration, and developing user-friendly applications. Challenges included potential sensor malfunctions, connectivity issues, and ensuring affordability for smallholder farmers. As a Civil engineer, My role involved designing and analyzing structural frameworks for sensor installations, ensuring durability in diverse environments, and optimizing layouts for efficient resource use in both soil-based and hydroponic systems. We also reviewed the Bill of Materials (BOM) to ensure cost efficiency and scalability and outlined procurement processes for sourcing reliable components. Additionally, we addressed user adoption hurdles and environmental factors, emphasizing modularity and sustainability to overcome these concerns.
